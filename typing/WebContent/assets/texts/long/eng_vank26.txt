Seokguram and Bulguksa, Masterpieces of Ancient Buddhist Art
Seokguram Grotto and Bulguksa Temple are examples of Buddhist sculpture and architecture, built in the 8th century during Silla (57 BC - AD 935).
They represent the essence of ancient Korea's Buddhist artwork, and they are considered masterpieces of Buddhist Art in Northeast Asia.
They are both located in Gyeongju, which was the capital of the millennium kingdom of Silla.
Gyeongju is home to many sculptures, temples, pagodas, royal tombs, a mountain fortress, and many other historical remains of Silla.
