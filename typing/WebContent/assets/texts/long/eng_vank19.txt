Changdeokgung Palace, Prime Example of Korean Traditional Architecture
Changdeokgung Palace is one of the five remaining palaces from the Joseon Dynasty (1392-1910), which is a prime example of Korean traditional architecture.
It was built in 1405 and inhabited by kings for the longest time.
Its asymmetrical layout and spacious garden show great harmony with the surrounding landscapes.
Harmony between human and nature is a key characteristic of Korean traditional architecture.
