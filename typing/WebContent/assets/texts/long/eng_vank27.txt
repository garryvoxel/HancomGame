Haeundae Beach, Famous Korean Sandy Beach
Haeundae Beach is a popular white sandy beach in Busan.
Its name originated from the pen name of Choi Chi-won (857-?), a renowned scholar of the Silla Kingdom (57 BC - AD 935).
The beach covers an area of 58 square kilometer with a total length of 1.5km.
Its water is shallow and safe, and its sand is soft.
Haeundae is a famous summer vacation destination.
Busan hosts many festivals, including the Busan International Film Festival, attracting tourists all year round.
