Mt. Halla, World Natural Heritage Site, Rich Repository of Ecosystems
Mt. Halla is a world natural heritage site and a rich repository of ecosystems where diverse species of animals and plants inhabit.
Located in the middle of the volcanic island of Jeju, it stands 1950 meters high, making it the tallest mountain in South Korea.
On the peak of Mt. Halla, you can look out over a beautiful natural landscape that contains various rare animals and plants, including endangered species.
Jeju is also home to many rare volcanos and lava caves.
